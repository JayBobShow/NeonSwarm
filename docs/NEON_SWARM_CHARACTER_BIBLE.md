# Neon Swarm Character Bible

## Phase 40 Character Lock

This document locks the core cast for Neon Swarm. These names and roles are the
official foundation for future dialogue, cutscenes, tutorial text, boss banter,
and story-facing UI.

## Nova Veyr

- Role: hero, player identity, former Rift Runner.
- Current form: soul and combat memory fused into the Aether Core.
- Story function: active protagonist, not a silent ship.
- Emotional drive: survive, remember, recover Mira, and protect the Grid without losing what remains of the self.
- Tone: focused, resilient, haunted, dry under pressure.

Nova was once a legendary Rift Runner who piloted the experimental Aether Core.
During the first Null invasion, Nova sacrificed their body to seal the breach.
Their mind, memory, and fighting instinct survived inside the Core.

Writing rule: Nova should sound like a person waking up inside a weapon, not a
generic pilot or arcade announcer.

## The Aether Core

- Role: player body and living geometric weapon.
- Nature: ancient light engine, memory vessel, combat platform.
- Visual identity: radiant geometric core, color, motion, ripple, weapon memory projection.
- Story function: the body Nova now inhabits.

The Aether Core is alive enough to hold memory, pain, instinct, and growth. It
does not replace Nova. It carries Nova.

## Lyra Quill

- Role: guide, engineer, survivor of Lumenfall.
- Personality: smart, sarcastic, loyal, practical, brave under stress.
- Systems explained: Armory, Forge, Neon Dust, upgrades, run weapons, sectors, and memory instability.
- Relationship to Nova: rescuer, technician, eventually trusted friend.

Lyra finds the Aether Core signal in the ruined Neon Grid and becomes Nova's
voice in the dark. She repairs what she can, jokes when afraid, and refuses to
let the Grid die quietly.

Example line:

"Nova, if this works, we live. If it does not, try to explode beautifully."

Writing rule: Lyra explains systems through urgency and personality. Avoid
tutorial text that sounds detached from the world.

## Rook-7

- Role: old defense machine, dry ally, memory witness.
- Personality: literal, protective, formal, unexpectedly loyal.
- Relationship to Nova: remembers Nova from before the collapse.
- Story function: proves Nova had a life before the Core and carries old-war context.

Rook-7 is an ancient Grid defense unit still running on damaged protocols. It
speaks like a machine, but its choices reveal attachment.

Example line:

"Combat odds unacceptable. Proceeding anyway."

Writing rule: Rook-7 should be concise, literal, and protective. Its emotional
beats work best when understated.

## Mira Sol

- Role: lost friend, emotional heart of the story, living lock mystery.
- Personality: warm, brave, luminous, quietly determined.
- Relationship to Nova: friend whose memory motivates Nova's recovery.
- Hidden truth: Mira is alive in some form inside the Prism Shards / Null prison mystery.
- Sector 4 reveal: Mira became the living lock holding back the Null King.

Mira's voice should arrive first as fragments, not exposition dumps. She is the
reason the story is about memory and meaning, not just survival.

Example line:

"If the Grid falls, promise me you will find the light under the dark."

Writing rule: Mira should not be treated as a reward object. She has agency,
sacrifice, fear, and resolve.

## The Null King

- Role: main villain.
- Title: The Shape That Eats Stars.
- Final boss title: The Null King, Crown of the Empty Grid.
- Personality: calm, ancient, cruel, certain.
- Visual identity: black crowned geometric force, white cracks, dead neon stars.
- Goal: erase color, memory, motion, life, and meaning.

The Null King does not rant. It speaks with quiet finality, as if entropy has a
voice.

Example line:

"All light flickers. All sound fades. All motion fails. I am the final shape."

Writing rule: The Null King should feel like the universe arguing for silence.

## Relationship Map

- Nova and Lyra: survival partnership that becomes trust.
- Nova and Rook-7: old-war recognition and machine loyalty.
- Nova and Mira: lost friendship, memory anchor, final emotional conflict.
- Lyra and Rook-7: practical engineer versus ancient machine logic, with mutual respect.
- Mira and the Null King: living lock versus erasure force.
- Nova and the Null King: motion, memory, and will against stillness and deletion.
