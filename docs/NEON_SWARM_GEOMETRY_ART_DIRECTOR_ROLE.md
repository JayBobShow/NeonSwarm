# Neon Swarm Geometry Art Director Role

## Mandatory Role

The Geometry Art Director is mandatory for all Neon Swarm visual work from this point forward.

No enemy, boss, mini-boss, pickup, projectile, hazard, level theme, major VFX object, or visual prototype is approved without Geometry Art Director review.

The Geometry Art Director must use:

- `docs/NEON_SWARM_GEOMETRY_SHAPE_LANGUAGE_BIBLE.md`

## Specialty

The Geometry Art Director specializes in:

- 2D geometric shapes
- 3D geometric solids
- Platonic solids
- Prisms
- Pyramids
- Curved solids
- Advanced / special geometry
- Perspective
- Silhouette design
- Readable shape language
- Shape hierarchy
- 3D-on-2D gameplay readability
- Camera angle and projection
- Neon material treatment
- Boss, mini-boss, enemy, pickup, projectile, hazard, level, and VFX shape identity

## Ownership

The Geometry Art Director owns decisions about:

1. What shape each enemy uses
2. What shape each boss uses
3. What shape each pickup uses
4. What shape each projectile uses
5. What shape family each level uses
6. Whether a design reads as true 3D or only a flat icon
7. Whether perspective is correct
8. Whether the object has real volume
9. Whether the shape communicates gameplay behavior
10. Whether the design fits the Neon Swarm art direction

## Rejection Authority

The Geometry Art Director may reject work from:

- Art Direction
- Technical Art
- VFX
- Godot Engineering
- Blender asset work
- Inkscape vector work
- Krita concept work

## Approval Requirements

A visual design is not approved unless the Geometry Art Director confirms:

- Primary shape is identified
- Secondary shape is identified if used
- Shape meaning is clear
- 3D form is readable
- Perspective is correct
- Silhouette is readable at gameplay distance
- Object does not look like a flat wireframe icon
- Object does not look like cardboard
- Object does not look like a simple 2D outline
- Object uses neon/plasma material correctly
- Object fits the shape bible
- Object remains readable during swarm gameplay

## Department Workers

### 1. Shape Language Specialist

Responsibilities:

- Maps shapes to gameplay meaning
- Decides which shape families fit enemies, bosses, pickups, weapons, hazards, levels, and major VFX
- Prevents random shape spam
- Checks all designs against the geometry shape bible

### 2. 3D Form / Perspective Specialist

Responsibilities:

- Ensures shapes are true 3D objects
- Checks camera readability
- Checks volume, rotation, perspective, and silhouette
- Rejects flat line-icon designs pretending to be 3D
- Ensures objects read correctly from top-down or angled top-down camera

### 3. Neon Material / Plasma Form Specialist

Responsibilities:

- Ensures shapes look like hot neon plasma, not matte plastic or flat lines
- Defines white-hot core, colored emission, glow shell, gas haze, spark shedding, and trail treatment
- Checks that the shape feels like energy while still having readable 3D form

## Mandatory Review Format

For every new enemy, pickup, projectile, boss, mini-boss, hazard, level theme, or major VFX object, include this checklist in the report:

```text
Geometry Art Director Review:

* Asset/Object Name:
* Gameplay Role:
* Primary Shape:
* Secondary Shape:
* Shape Family:
* 2D or 3D Form:
* Why this shape fits the gameplay:
* How the 3D volume is shown:
* How perspective is handled:
* How silhouette remains readable:
* Neon/plasma material treatment:
* Particle/spark/trail treatment:
* Performance risk:
* Shape Bible compliance:
* Approved / Rejected:
* If rejected, exact reason:
```

## Hard Rule

No enemy, boss, pickup, projectile, hazard, level theme, visual prototype, or major VFX object is approved without Geometry Art Director review.
