# Neon Swarm Enemy Faction Bible

## Phase 40 Faction Lock

This document locks the official enemy faction naming foundation for Neon Swarm.
It does not add new enemy behavior. It gives current and future enemies a world
identity for UI text, bestiary work, encounter design, visual direction, and
story implementation.

## The Swarm

The Swarm is the umbrella threat created by Null corruption. It includes broken
glass fragments, prism ghosts, corrupted Grid machines, and direct Nullborn
elite forms.

The Swarm is what matter, memory, and machine logic become when the Null King
tears meaning out of them.

## Faction 1: Shardlings

Shardlings are small basic Swarm enemies, broken fragments of corrupted neon
glass. They are the earliest and most common expression of the Swarm.

Core traits:

- Fast, sharp, fragile, numerous.
- Built from broken light, glass, and motion.
- Best used for early pressure and readable swarm density.

Example enemy names:

- Red Shardling
- Blue Cutter Shard
- Violet Skitter
- Splinter Drone

Current-game mapping guidance:

- Chaser-style pressure can be flavored as Shardlings.
- Dash or cutter variants can become Cutter Shards or Violet Skitters.
- Split fragments can use Splinter Drone naming.

Visual language:

- Small shards.
- Triangles, splinters, diamonds, broken glass edges.
- Red, blue, violet, cyan, and magenta corruption glints.

## Faction 2: Prism Wraiths

Prism Wraiths are ghost-like enemies from Sector 2. They flicker through prism
cracks and attack in angled patterns.

Core traits:

- Unstable, refracted, deceptive.
- Strong association with Prism Rift and Mira memory fragments.
- Movement should feel like reflected light and broken geometry.

Example enemy names:

- Prism Wraith
- Glass Howler
- Rift Shade
- Mirror Stalker

Current-game mapping guidance:

- Prism Leech-style hazard pressure belongs here.
- Fractured Sector 2 enemies can be renamed into this family later.
- Mirror / phase enemies should use this faction before inventing new groups.

Visual language:

- Purple, magenta, violet, cyan highlights.
- Diamond, prism, glass crack, mirror shard silhouettes.
- Flicker, refraction, angled lane attacks.

## Faction 3: Gridborn Machines

Gridborn Machines are old security machines corrupted by the Null King. They
were built to defend the Neon Grid, but now enforce broken protocols.

Core traits:

- Mechanical, rail-based, procedural, defensive.
- Best used for old-system pressure, turrets, rails, anchors, and security gates.
- They can sound official even when hostile.

Example enemy names:

- Rail Skimmer
- Pulse Turret
- Grid Splitter
- Circuit Hound
- Anchor Drone

Current-game mapping guidance:

- Existing Rail Skimmer, Grid Splitter, and Grid Fragment concepts belong here.
- Sector events can use Gridborn naming for broken systems reconnecting.
- Future factory-sector enemies in Ember Circuit should lean Gridborn.

Visual language:

- Rectangles, rails, hard panels, antennae, machinery joints.
- Cyan, blue, white, orange warning lights.
- Clean lines corrupted by black gaps or broken neon seams.

## Faction 4: Nullborn

Nullborn are elite enemies directly created by the Null King. They are not
broken things. They are deliberate shapes of erasure.

Core traits:

- Elite, cold, silent, dangerous.
- Directly tied to the Null King, Black Crown, and late-game pressure.
- Should feel less alive and more inevitable.

Example enemy names:

- Null Leech
- Void Spear
- Black Crown Fragment
- Silence Knight
- Hollow Vector

Current-game mapping guidance:

- Null Octagon-family threats are legacy prototypes of Nullborn boss language.
- Future Black Crown elites should use Nullborn names.
- Do not overuse Nullborn in early sectors.

Visual language:

- Black geometry, white cracks, dead neon, crown fragments.
- Octagons, void spears, hollow vectors, negative space.
- Minimal motion until sudden precise attacks.

## Naming Rules

- Do not use placeholder names for story-facing enemies once a faction name exists.
- Use Shardlings for basic swarm enemies.
- Use Prism Wraiths for Sector 2 prism ghosts and glass-memory threats.
- Use Gridborn Machines for corrupted old defense systems.
- Use Nullborn for direct Null King creations and late elite threats.
- Avoid making every enemy "Null" flavored. Nullborn should feel special.
