# Neon Swarm Active Art Direction

Neon Swarm is a fast 3D-on-2D neon arena survival shooter. Gameplay remains
readable on a flat combat plane while important objects present as real 3D
geometric forms.

## Approved Direction

Core look:

- Dark 3D geometry with visible volume.
- Bright neon tube edges, seams, rings, corners, and accents.
- White-hot cores or vertices where gameplay importance needs emphasis.
- Clean silhouette hierarchy at gameplay camera distance.
- Strong projectile readability.
- High contrast between player, enemies, bullets, XP, bosses, HUD, and floors.
- Sector-specific arena identity without sacrificing combat clarity.
- Geometric bosses with distinct silhouettes, telegraphs, and projectile reads.

Visual priority in busy gameplay:

1. Player.
2. Enemy danger.
3. Enemy bullets and boss attacks.
4. Player bullets and weapon effects.
5. XP and rewards.
6. HUD.
7. Arena and background.

Arena direction:

- Sector arenas should feel authored, not random.
- Floors need visible material and edge structure, not black voids with lines.
- Borders, rails, panels, and depth geometry should clarify the playable arena.
- Decorative architecture must not imply false playable routes.

Enemy and boss direction:

- Every enemy needs a primary geometry identity.
- Bosses should read as larger, more deliberate geometric machines/entities.
- Motion, color, silhouette, and attack tells must stay distinct.
- Effects should support the shape read rather than replace it.

Projectile and VFX direction:

- Projectiles need clear direction, source, threat ownership, and impact.
- Trails should communicate motion without filling the screen.
- Glow should be local and controlled.
- Hit flashes, bursts, sparks, and haze must clear quickly.

## Rejected Styles

Do not use:

- Toy-like assets.
- Flat placeholder shapes presented as final art.
- Matte plastic primitives.
- Cardboard-looking geometry.
- Muddy glow or screen-wide neon haze.
- Unreadable black-on-black floors.
- Cluttered VFX that hides gameplay.
- Generic enemies without distinct shape language.
- Random line overlays instead of authored arena surfaces.
- Tiny invisible enemies.
- Weak projectiles that disappear in combat.
- HUD clutter that fights the playfield.

## Production Standard

Visual work is not approved because it looks good in isolation, has many
objects, has a render, or passes an asset-count check. It is approved only when
it reads in the official game at gameplay camera distance and preserves combat
clarity.
